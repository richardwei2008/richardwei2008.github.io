I. Have a Clear Strategic Focus

Performance with Purpose

- "Good for you" products

- Product innovations

- Emerging markets

PepsiCo hit some bumps (2010)

Coca-Cola's rose by 40%

Made key management changes

Stayed with strategic shift

PepsiCo achieved solid growth (2013)

PepsiCo's revenue and earnings per share rose by 1 % and 10%

Coca-Cola's declined by 2% and 3%

II. Analyze Your Business as an Activist Would

Analyzing how the activists might try to increase short-term shareholder value

- spin-offs and divestitures or financial engineering

-> stock buybacks

-> increased debt

- the company's possible vulnerabilities in strategy and capital structure

III. Have Your External Advisers Lined Up in Advance

Bill Ackman's Pershing Square hedge fund (2007 and 2008)

- persuading the Target board to spin off its credit card operations

- proposing seperate its real estate operations

- launching a proxy fight to replace long-standing board members

External advisers that rely on

- financial adviser (Goldman Sachs)

- legal counsel (Wachtell, Lipton)

IV. Build Board Chemistry

- repeated engagement on important issues

- weathering crises together

- candid dialogue with the CEO

V. Perform in the Short Run Against Declared Goals

- consistent performance that realizes the company's stated goals

VI. Don't Dismiss Activist Ideas Out of Hand

- most activist investors are smart, motivated people

- often notice things that boards and managers overlook

- worth listening to their recommendations

- and implementing the ones that make sense

Doing What's Best for All your Shareholders

- stay true to the mission and value
